I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of California, Berkeley, affiliated with the School of Information and Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research (BAIR). I am also pursuing an M.S. in Computer Science through UC Berkeley EECS, and a minor in Computational Precision Health via a joint program with the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF). I am co-advised by Dr. Ahmed Alaa and Dr. David Bamman.

My research develops and applies machine learning methods for clinical reasoning and disease progression modeling using unstructured text and longitudinal time series data from electronic health records. My work spans language technologies for medicine, multi-step biomedical and clinical reasoning, and longitudinal patient modeling.

I received my master's degree from the University of Pennsylvania, and bachelor's degree from Claremont McKenna College. I previously interned with the Health Futures team at Microsoft Research (MSR).
